About Costa Rica

Costa Rica, meaning "Rich Coast" in Spanish, is a Central American country known for its incredible biodiversity and commitment to environmental conservation. With coastlines on both the Caribbean Sea and Pacific Ocean, it offers stunning beaches, lush rainforests, and active volcanoes.

The country abolished its army in 1949 and has since invested heavily in education and healthcare. Over 25% of its land is protected in national parks and reserves, making it a premier ecotourism destination.

Top Attractions

Arenal Volcano

A mystical forest reserve with incredible biodiversity and hanging bridges.

Manuel Antonio National Park

Beautiful beaches, hiking trails, and abundant wildlife including monkeys and sloths.

Tortuguero National Park

Known as the "Amazon of Costa Rica," accessible only by boat or plane.
